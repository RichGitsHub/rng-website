WHEELS

A screenplay based on Roller Hockey International

WHEELS is a narrative screenplay drawn from the rise and fall of Roller Hockey International — a league fueled by ambition, money, near-misses, and the belief that the next break was always one shift away.

The story follows players, promoters, and dreamers caught between sport and spectacle, loyalty and opportunity, and the cost of chasing something just out of reach.

A note on the work

A couple of years ago, I made the difficult decision to put my WHEELS screenplay on the back burner. I had spent three years working on it, on and off, and while there’s strong material in it, much of it was written in the wrong format. It leaned toward a director’s script — and directors don’t tend to welcome direction, especially from a novice screenwriter.

I’ve since returned to the project with a clearer sense of the form and the discipline it demands. I’m actively working on WHEELS again now, and I expect to have a sharp, finished script before too much longer.

An excerpt from the screenplay

49 INT. RINK IN LOS ANGELES. TRYOUT CAMP. DAY. COACH BOBBY HULL, JR. There are hundreds of guys trying out today. If you’re not selected by the Los Angeles Landsharks, it’s not a slam on you. Most of the team has already been picked, between players already on the team, and those selected in the draft. If any of you make it, you should be proud. PLAYER WEARING ROLLERBLADES That doesn’t sound too promising. COACH BOBBY HULL, JR. If you can’t score, hit somebody. If you can’t hit somebody, score! ASSISTANT COACH And if you can’t do either, coach. COACH BOBBY HULL, JR. I heard that. ASSISTANT COACH OK, first line of red jerseys scrimmage against first line of blue jerseys. Go! COACH BOBBY HULL, JR. Good thing our team is already mostly set. Some of these guys are delusional if they think they’re pro caliber. ASSISTANT COACH Got that right. Look at Bambi over there. COACH BOBBY HULL, JR. There’s one guy I’ve got my eye on for a forward. See that kid over there? He’s making some impressive passes and scoring some skillful goals. Holy crap! He’s in a fight! And he’s holding his own. ASSISTANT COACH I’d say. The other guy is big, but he’s turtling. He’s done.
